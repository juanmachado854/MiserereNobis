Apologetics

This section of Miserere Nobis is dedicated to Catholic apologetics — offering reasons for faith, answering common questions, and drawing from the Church’s intellectual tradition.

The Five Ways of St. Thomas Aquinas

St. Thomas Aquinas presented five philosophical arguments for the existence of God: motion, causation, contingency, degrees of perfection, and teleology (design). Each begins with observation of the natural world and reasons upward to a necessary First Cause.

The Eucharist and the Real Presence

Catholic teaching holds that in the Mass, bread and wine truly become the Body and Blood of Christ. This doctrine, known as the Real Presence, has been affirmed since the earliest centuries and is grounded in Scripture, tradition, and the witness of the saints.

Mary and the Communion of Saints

Catholics honor Mary as the Mother of God and venerate the saints, not as gods, but as members of the Body of Christ who intercede for us. This reflects the scriptural truth that death does not sever the unity of the faithful in Christ.
